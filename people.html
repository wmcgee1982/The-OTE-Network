People

Born from the desire for inclusivity, we at Out There Exeter ignites connection and celebration within our vibrant LGBTQIA+ community. We move with respect, empowering voices and lifting spirits. Our team is the beating pulse of a movement, many stories woven into a tapestry of shared culture. Hand in hand, we are reimagining Exeter's queer landscape. Fellow us, and join our journey.

Our governance structure consists of the following roles:

  • Trustee Board
  • Voting Members
  • Non-Voting Associates
  • Committee groups

Trustees

The Trustee Board have the legal responsibility for governing and managing Out There Exeter. Trustees are responsible for ensuring that the charity operates in accordance with its governing document and the law, and that it achieves its charitable objectives.

Trustees are elected at AGMs, are unpaid and use their skills and experience to set Out There Exeter's strategy and objectives and help the organisation meet them. Trustees must abide by and agree to the Trustee Terms and Conditions (T&Cs).

Members

Out There Exeter defines its members as active members who have voting rights, as outlined in the constitution. To become a member, one must sign up and agree to the Membership T&Cs. Having membership brings real benefits to Out There Exeter. Members help us shape our agenda, refresh the trustee body, and improve accountability. Effective membership requires members to vote in Out There Exeter's best interests, and for trustees to manage membership properly. This includes adapting the membership structure as Out There Exeter grows. We recognise that Out There Exeter must balance membership involvement with trustee responsibility.

Associates

Associate members of Out There Exeter support the organisation without having voting rights, formal privileges, or legal obligations. It'ss a great way to get involved and meet other people who are passionate about the same causes, without having to make a major commitment. The rights of, and conditions for, Associates are set- out in our Membership T&Cs.

Committees

Committees help manage and run Out There Exeter. They are entrusted by the trustees to undertake specific tasks, functions, or areas of work, such as fundraising, events, marketing, strategy, or governance. The delegation of these functions is determined by the Committee T&Cs and Committee Terms of Reference (TORs).

Meetings

The following table provides an overview of the meeting attendance, voting rights, and delegated powers for the charity's Trustees, Members, Associates, and Committees:

Role Meeting Attendance Voting Rights Delegated Powers
Trustees Required to attend all Trustee meetings, general meetings, and Annual General Meetings (AGM) Full voting rights on all matters Authority to make decisions on behalf of the charity within the limits of the charity's governing documents
Members Encouraged to attend the Annual General Meeting (AGM) and any other general meetings Some voting rights on matters as stipulated within the constitution and membership T&Cs No authority to make decisions
Associates Encouraged to attend general meetings but not required to do so No voting rights No authority to make decisions
Committees Members of committees are required to attend committee meetings Full voting rights on matters within the committee's terms of reference Authority to make decisions on behalf of the charity within the limits of the committee's terms of reference and subject to the approval of the Trustees
