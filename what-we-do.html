What We Do

Our mission is to strengthen connections between communities and groups to celebrate LGBTQIA+ culture and work to create an inclusive future for LGBTQIA+ people.

Our aim, to enrich and advance Exeter's LGBTQIA+ cultural landscape and all branches of the community across the city, can be achieved through four tangible goals:

One: Stronger Relationships

Support initiatives that encourage community participation through fostering stronger relationships between LGBTQIA+ members and the wider communities across Exeter.

Two: Local Directory

Create and promote an LGBTQIA+ directory of groups, partners, mentors, programmes, and opportunities that support LGBTQIA+ people to thrive, connect them with relevant supportive resources and networks, and improve their conditions of life within Exeter.

Three: Annual Festival

Host an annual festival in collaboration with LGBTQIA+ organisations and individuals, to profile and raise awareness of the culture, support, and opportunities available within the communities across Exeter.

Four: Recreational Opportunities

Provide year-round recreational opportunities for LGBTQIA+ groups to build a sense of belonging and make Exeter a more inclusive and welcoming place to live, work and visit.

Progress so far...

We've been quietly hustling away in our most productive pants to get the first festival up and running.

The festival is currently pencilled in for 22nd-29th September 2024.

Queer Fest (working title) is a two-part format: a week dedicated to showcasing community organisations, advocacy initiatives, and diverse culture, culminating in a vibrant weekend of arts and music. We have taken a deliberate approach of starting with programming achievable and realistic, that we will enrich with additional experiences - resources, funding and partnership permitting.

All plans are still to be confirmed, but here is a sneak peek at our proposal.

  • Friday: an immersive late-night of culture and arts at the RAMM.
  • Friday & Saturday Evening: an intimate cabaret at the Mermaid.
  • Saturday: a day of entertainment followed by a headliner at the Phoenix.
  • For a calmer but equally fun scene, an alternative program at the library, for the Saturday.
  • Sunday: a holistic experience at the RAMM, followed by a panel discussion and band at the Phoenix.
Working Group

We have a festival working group formed of event leads who meet regularly. Our members and associates are updated with progress in the form of monthly newsletters and quarterly general meetings. The next general meeting will be March'24, further details to be confirmed.
